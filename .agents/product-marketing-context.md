# Product Marketing Context

*Last updated: 2026-04-05*

## Product Overview
**One-liner:** Men's topical peptide skincare bundles positioned as performance optimization tools — not beauty products.
**What it does:** Peptide Protocol sells bundled skincare kits built around clinical-grade peptide compounds and tools. Each bundle is a complete system targeting a specific outcome (daily maintenance, facial definition, nighttime repair, full AM/PM coverage, or post-weight-loss volume restoration). Products are sold as one-time purchases or subscriptions with auto-refills.
**Product category:** Men's skin performance / optimization (NOT men's grooming or skincare)
**Product type:** DTC e-commerce, subscription-optional bundles
**Business model:** One-time bundle purchases ($79-$169) + subscription refills ($55-$99/mo). ~77-88% margins. Hosted on GitHub Pages, ads via Meta.

## Target Audience

### Persona A: "The Optimizer" — Men 27-38
**Income:** $75K-$150K
**Psychographic:** Biohacker-adjacent. Already buys performance supplements, fitness tech, optimization tools. Thinks in systems. Discovered looksmaxxing concepts 2-5 years ago but aged out of the community. Wants science-backed, not trend-driven.
**Platform behavior:** Heavy Instagram Reels, YouTube Shorts, TikTok. Responds to mechanism-of-action language. Wants to know WHY something works.
**Purchase triggers:** Data, clinical references, visible protocols, before/after texture comparisons
**Ad tone:** "Here's how this works at a cellular level, and here's the protocol."

### Persona B: "The Reclaimer" — Men 38-50
**Income:** $100K-$250K+
**Psychographic:** High earner who just noticed he looks tired. Googling "how to look younger" privately. Has never had a skincare routine. Wants to be told what to do. Decision fatigue is real — he wants ONE system, not 40 product options. Will pay premium for simplicity and credibility.
**Platform behavior:** Facebook, Instagram feed (less Reels), YouTube pre-roll. Responds to outcome-based language and social proof from men who look like him — professionals, not influencers.
**Purchase triggers:** Simplicity ("3 products, 2 minutes"), spousal/social validation ("people will notice"), time efficiency
**Ad tone:** "Stop overthinking it. Here's the routine. It works."

## Problems & Pain Points

### Optimizer (27-38) Pain Points — Lead with these
- **Collagen decline is already happening:** "You're losing 1% collagen per year after 25." They don't realize the degradation has started. The fear is invisible decay — they're optimizing everything else but ignoring the one thing people see first.
- **Information overload, no protocol:** They've researched peptides, retinoids, acids — but there's no structured system. They're stuck in analysis paralysis with 40 browser tabs open.
- **Aging out of looksmaxxing:** They discovered these concepts but the communities are for 19-year-olds. They want the science without the culture.
- **Face doesn't match the body:** They train hard, eat clean, supplement — but their face tells a different story. It's the one system they haven't optimized.

### Reclaimer (38-50) Pain Points — Lead with these
- **Looking tired when you're not:** The "worn out" look that won't go away. Inflammation, slack skin, undefined jawline. People at work assume you're exhausted or aging fast.
- **Wife/partner noticed, or you noticed in photos:** The private moment of "when did I start looking old?" Often triggered by a photo, a video call, or a comment.
- **Post-weight-loss face collapse:** Lost 30-40lbs (often GLP-1 meds) and now the face looks deflated, hollow, older. "Everyone told you to lose the weight. Nobody told you what it would do to your face."
- **Decision fatigue:** He has zero skincare knowledge and no interest in becoming an expert. He wants to be TOLD what to do. One box, one protocol card, done.
- **Lost jawline/definition:** "You didn't lose your jawline. It's under fluid and slack skin." The structure is still there — it's masked by fluid retention and tissue laxity.

**Why alternatives fall short:**
- Traditional men's grooming brands are surface-level (moisturizer + face wash) with no compound-level targeting
- Women's skincare brands overwhelm with 10-step routines and terminology that doesn't resonate
- Looksmaxxing communities are trend-driven and unstructured — no curated protocols
- Dermatologists prescribe individual products, not systems — and cost 10x more
- Doing nothing: the default. The cost is compounding — every month without intervention is another month of collagen loss

**What it costs them:** Looking tired, aged, or undefined when they're otherwise optimizing every other system in their life. Professional credibility. Social confidence. The way their partner looks at them.
**Emotional tension:** Private insecurity about aging appearance. Men don't talk about this. They Google it at midnight. They compare themselves in the mirror. They notice it on Zoom calls. The entry point is always pain — the product is the exit.

## Competitive Landscape
**Direct:** Tiege Hanley, Lumin, Brickell — falls short because they sell generic routines without compound-level targeting or system thinking
**Secondary:** Individual peptide serums from The Ordinary, NIOD — falls short because they're individual products requiring the user to build their own protocol
**Indirect:** Botox, fillers, dermatology visits — falls short because they're expensive, invasive, require ongoing appointments, and don't address the root system

## Differentiation
**Key differentiators:**
- System-based approach: bundles are complete protocols, not product collections
- Compound-level messaging: every product explained by mechanism of action
- Objective-specific bundles: each one targets a defined outcome (definition, repair, rebuild)
- Male-coded positioning: clinical, utilitarian, zero spa energy
- Tools included: derma rollers, contour tools, cryo rollers, red light masks, mastic gum — not just serums
**How we do it differently:** We sell outcomes via complete systems. You pick the objective, we give you the protocol.
**Why that's better:** No research, no decision fatigue, no 40-product shelf. One box, one card, one protocol.
**Why customers choose us:** Simplicity + credibility + specificity. It's the only brand that speaks to men the way they think — in systems, compounds, and protocols.
