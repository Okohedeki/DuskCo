# PEPTIDE PROTOCOL BRAND — AD CAMPAIGN BUILD

## CONTEXT
I'm launching a men's topical peptide skincare brand selling bundled product kits positioned as performance optimization tools — not beauty products. I need to build out ad campaigns for 5 product bundles, each tested against 2 target demographics simultaneously. I have an ads MCP connected. Build everything needed to launch these campaigns.

---

## BRAND VOICE & POSITIONING
- **Category:** Men's skin performance / optimization
- **Tone:** Clinical, direct, zero fluff. Think Whoop meets Aesop. Never say "glow," "radiant," "pamper," or "self-care." Use words like: protocol, system, rebuild, compound, signal, repair, remodel, defend.
- **Visual direction:** Dark, clean, utilitarian. Matte black packaging. Product-in-use photography style — hands doing things, not lifestyle portraits. No spa energy. Think tool bench, not bathroom vanity.
- **Core brand promise:** "Your face is a system. Treat it like one."

## TARGET DEMOGRAPHICS

### Demo A: "The Optimizer" — Men 27-38
- **Income:** $75K-$150K
- **Psychographic:** Biohacker-adjacent. Already buys performance supplements, fitness tech, optimization tools. Thinks in systems. Discovered looksmaxxing concepts 2-5 years ago but aged out of the community. Wants science-backed, not trend-driven.
- **Platform behavior:** Heavy Instagram Reels, YouTube Shorts, TikTok. Responds to mechanism-of-action language. Wants to know WHY something works, not just that it does.
- **Purchase triggers:** Data, clinical references, visible protocols, before/after texture (not wrinkle) comparisons
- **Ad tone:** "Here's how this works at a cellular level, and here's the protocol."

### Demo B: "The Reclaimer" — Men 38-50
- **Income:** $100K-$250K+
- **Psychographic:** High earner who just noticed he looks tired. Googling "how to look younger" privately. Has never had a skincare routine. Wants to be told what to do. Decision fatigue is real — he wants ONE system, not 40 product options. Will pay premium for simplicity and credibility.
- **Platform behavior:** Facebook, Instagram feed (less Reels), YouTube pre-roll. Responds to outcome-based language and social proof from men who look like him — professionals, not influencers.
- **Purchase triggers:** Simplicity ("3 products, 2 minutes"), spousal/social validation ("people will notice"), time efficiency
- **Ad tone:** "Stop overthinking it. Here's the routine. It works."

## CAMPAIGN STRUCTURE

### Ad Sets (10 total):
For each of the 5 bundles, create 2 ad sets:
1. Demo A targeting (Men 27-38, interests: biohacking, fitness tech, supplements, men's health, longevity)
2. Demo B targeting (Men 38-50, interests: men's grooming, weight loss, executive health, golf, business)

### Per Ad Set:
- 3 ad creatives minimum (video, carousel, static)
- 2-3 hook variations per creative
- Landing page or product page URL (placeholder until built)
- Conversion objective: Purchase
- Daily budget: $50-75 per ad set for testing phase

### Testing Protocol:
- **Phase 1 (Days 1-7):** All 10 ad sets live. Broad creative testing.
- **Kill signal:** Any ad set below 1.5x ROAS by Day 7 gets creative refreshed, not killed yet.
- **Phase 2 (Days 8-14):** Cut any ad set still below 2x ROAS. Double budget on anything above 3x.
- **Phase 3 (Day 15+):** Scale winners. Kill losers. Begin creative iteration on top 2-3 performers.

### Key Metrics to Track:
- CPA per bundle per demo
- CTR per hook variation
- AOV (are people buying bundles or individual products?)
- Subscription conversion rate (what % opt into recurring?)
- ROAS at Day 7, Day 14, Day 30
